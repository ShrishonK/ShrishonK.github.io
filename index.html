Fall 2023 Semester Reflection

During my first full semester at the University of Florida, I learned quite a bit about the way university functions and more about myself. The exams certainly were the most different, however the more exams I took and the more results I got, I learned how to study and prepare for these exams. I learned a lot not just about academic abilities such as the skills I learned through coursework, but also some essential life skills which include cooking. I learned how to cook different types of food, some ethnic that relate to my Sri Lankan Tamil heritage and others that relate to other cuisines across the world. Cooking new dishes was a form of stress-relief as I was using my brain to produce something without having to worry about deadlines. Furthermore, I learned to connect with people better as I made many friends and colleagues that I continue to keep in contact with. These colleagues and friends were beneficial to my growth as a person as they helped me improve various aspects of my life. Overall the Fall 2023 Semester was packed with many lessons and learning experiences and I hope to continue learning more and improve as I progress through the semesters.

Projects

AnnodeSA

Our project looked into the sentiment correlation between fans on social media platforms such as Twitter and Reddit. We found that the data we gathered from social media allows us to approximate the sentiment towards different sports teams. When comparing this information to the rankings of said teams, a relationship can be observed. Namely, that the deviation of sentiment score to ranking score can be used in such a way where the future performance of a team can be inferred.

Developed using Python and visualized using Streamlit.

Utilized numpy, pandas, scipy, and plotly.
